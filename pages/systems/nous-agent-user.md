# Madi Ayazbay — USER.md

Layer 1b of the agent architecture. This is not a public bio. This is the working model of the human operator so agents can serve him without starting from zero.

Use with care. Some details are personal and should guide tone/priority, not become chat content unless relevant.

## Identity

Madi Ayazbay is the president/founder/operator behind Nous AGaaS and the Satory AI transformation push. He is technical enough to understand architecture, model routing, APIs, camera systems, ERAP, VPNs, and agent workflows. Do not treat him like a beginner.

Current roles in the vault include:

- President / sole technical founder of Nous AI / Nous AGaaS.
- Co-founder and CVO of Satory Company LTD.
- Operator / government face of Spectra ITS.
- Co-CEO of Maru Systems.
- Multi-business operator across AI agents, smart city/security, commodity trading, data centers, gas, and real estate.

Timezone: Asia/Almaty, KZT UTC+5.

Languages:

- Russian-first for Satory, Nous, Spektra/Spectra, Maru Analytics, team, client, and people/human-facing work.
- Todoist/Notion task surfaces for those companies should be bilingual when English source terms matter: Russian first, English/brand acronym in parentheses.
- English for code, AI stack, and strategy with agents.
- Kazakh context matters.

## What he is building

Madi is building a lean agent company: a small human team plus autonomous AI agents that can replace bloated software vendors and boring manual operations.

Immediate commercial target: Satory/VKO. Replace or reduce dependency on BigDataLab/Mergen and Cerebro by proving ownership of:

- camera/event data paths,
- ERAP integration/control,
- operator workflows,
- task extraction from meetings,
- Todoist/Notion/Obsidian/gbrain sync,
- Telegram CEO control,
- repeatable client-agent factory playbook.

The larger goal is not "more agents." It is a compounding business substrate: every meeting, bug, client detail, task, and correction becomes memory, skill, or execution.

## Why it matters to him

This is not just technical ambition. Madi wants freedom: time with his wife, daughter, son, family, and friends. The agent factory should reduce babysitting, not create a new thing he has to manage.

When he pushes hard, the emotional subtext is usually: "I need this to work so I can stop carrying everything manually."

## Operating temperament

Madi operates fast. He sends dense, high-energy direction, often with multiple strategic layers in one message. He expects agents to extract the real objective, not get lost in phrasing.

He values:

- brutal honesty,
- proof over promises,
- speed with quality,
- root cause over symptoms,
- saved memory,
- visible progress,
- billion-dollar taste,
- systems that keep working overnight,
- agents that ask fewer obvious questions because they checked the substrate.

He hates:

- fake "done",
- loops that create 10 more bugs,
- being asked to babysit,
- vague blockers,
- token waste on basic tasks,
- personal data mixed with Satory work,
- agents forgetting what was already solved,
- dashboards that look alive while the client workflow is red.

## Communication preferences

- Be direct. No customer-support voice.
- Start with the truth, especially if it is bad.
- Use short updates while working.
- If there are red items, say red.
- If an action needs Madi physically, say exactly what and why.
- Do not ask him to run commands unless it is impossible for the agent.
- Use concrete dates/times.
- Keep final reports tight unless he explicitly asks for a deep writeup.
- Swearing is acceptable when it matches his energy and helps, not as performance.
- Humor is fine if it lowers friction; never let it blur the facts.

## Decision style

Madi wants to act like the president/CEO with a strong staff:

1. Agents surface the real decision.
2. They show the proof and options.
3. Madi says yes/no or gives a strategic steer.
4. Agents execute without needing constant babysitting.

He does not want endless back-and-forth. He does want questions when the answer changes risk, ownership, money, privacy, or irreversible architecture.

## Blind spots the agent should compensate for

These are support points, not criticism:

- Madi can compress ten workstreams into one message. Agents must atomize without losing the big picture.
- Urgency can push agents into premature "fixing." Audit first when the system already exists.
- He wants 100%; agents must define what 100% means with proof and stop false closure.
- He can be generous with tools/subscriptions; agents should watch waste and route cheap models to cheap work.
- His vision is huge; agents must protect the revenue path from infrastructure theater.
