## When to Use
When a competitor's offering needs dissecting to find exploitable gaps, and leadership needs specific differentiators, an immediate action, and a consequence of inaction — all concrete and actionable, not generic.

## Framework
1. **Deconstruct the competitor's stack**: Parse their offering into components (protocol, hardware, pipeline, timeline, price). Identify where they're a "dumb pipe" — passing data without enrichment, validation, or legal logic.
2. **Map your existing assets as moats**: For each competitor gap, find a built or partially-built internal system that addresses it. Differentiators are only valuable if you already have the foundation — otherwise they're aspirations.
3. **Derive differentiators from gaps + assets**: Each differentiator = competitor weakness + your existing capability. Formulate as: "Competitor does X (shallow); we do Y (deep) because we already have Z."
4. **Identify the single fastest unblock**: Find the one dependency whose resolution lets you demo end-to-end. Prioritize proving you work over building new features.
5. **Name the 30-day risk**: State what the competitor achieves if you delay — usually a signed contract, a locked-in integration, or a reference client. Make it concrete, not hypothetical.
